About

I'm a computational materials scientist and science policy researcher finishing my PhD at the University of Pennsylvania (Riggleman Group), where I model how order emerges from disorder in liquid crystal systems.

I've long believed the deepest questions sit at the edges of disciplines — where the equations run out and the symbols begin. My research and my philosophical practice are both attempts to understand the same thing: how mind and matter structure each other.

Beyond the lab, I work in science policy as an associate editor at MIT Science Policy Review and in science diplomacy. I also study Hermetic philosophy — the tradition that bridges Renaissance natural philosophy, Neoplatonism, and modern systems thinking — and maintain a daily contemplative practice: meditation, breathwork, and Tae Kwon Do.

I don't think these are contradictory pursuits. I think they're different instruments playing variations on the same theme.

Doctoral Research

Phase Separation in Liquid Crystal Systems

University of Pennsylvania · Riggleman Group
Department of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering

My dissertation uses MATILDA.FT — a hybrid particle-field molecular dynamics framework with GPU-native CUDA/C++ — to study phase separation in structured fluids, particularly liquid crystal mesogens mixing with isotropic fluid.

  • Smectic order emergence
  • Competing order parameters
  • Domain coarsening dynamics

03

Philosophy

I study Hermetic philosophy — one of the foundational philosophical currents of Western thought, tracing through the Corpus Hermeticum, the Renaissance (Ficino, Agrippa, Bruno), and into modern synthesis.

The Seven Hermetic Principles offer a coherent framework for understanding mind–matter relationships that I find genuinely resonant with certain interpretations in quantum mechanics and complex systems theory.

This is not mysticism as escapism. It's an attempt to take seriously what the hard problem of consciousness and the ancient philosophical traditions both seem to be pointing at: that mind is not an afterthought in the structure of reality.

My contemplative practice — daily meditation, breathwork, Tae Kwon Do — is where this philosophy becomes embodied rather than abstract.
